Research

Exploring the fundamental principles of chemistry through computational and experimental approaches

Research Interests

Inorganic Chemistry

Investigating transition metal complexes, coordination chemistry, and metal-organic frameworks for catalytic applications.

Physical Chemistry

Studying thermodynamics, kinetics, and spectroscopy to understand molecular behavior and reaction mechanisms.

Computational Chemistry

Using quantum mechanical calculations and molecular dynamics simulations to predict molecular properties and reactions.

Materials Chemistry

Designing and synthesizing novel materials with tailored properties for energy storage and conversion applications.

Magnetochemistry

Exploring magnetic properties of lanthanide coordination compounds using ORCA DFT calculations. Focus on dysprosium triangle complexes and anisotropic magnetism for applications in single-molecule magnets.

Quantum Chemistry

Applying quantum mechanical principles to understand electronic structure and chemical bonding at the molecular level.

Current Research

01

Computational Magnetochemistry of Lanthanide Complexes

Computational Chemistry | Ongoing

Investigation of magnetic properties in lanthanide coordination compounds using ORCA DFT calculations. The research focuses on dysprosium triangle complexes and their anisotropic magnetic behavior, with applications toward single-molecule magnets and magnetic data storage materials.

ORCA DFT Lanthanides Magnetochemistry Anisotropic Magnetism
02

Molecular Dynamics of Copper-Organic Interfacial Systems

Computational Chemistry | Ongoing

Molecular dynamics simulations using LAMMPS to study copper-organic interfacial interactions with benzene and ethanol systems. Employing OPLS-AA and TRAPPE-UA force fields with MSM and PPPM electrostatics methods to investigate surface interactions and interfacial behavior at the molecular level.

LAMMPS Molecular Dynamics OPLS-AA TRAPPE-UA Interfacial Chemistry
